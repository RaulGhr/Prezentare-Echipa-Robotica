International Science Week

26.04.2019

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ This event started as a thought, how could we do something that would have an impact in the society by finding solutions to real-life problems, while also getting teams from all around the globe to collaborate with each other and to form new friendships.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ We immediately thought about a live discussion that could be seen live and in which everyone that: wanted could be a part of, but we didn't know any platforms that could sustain that many people video- chatting at the same time.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ The second idea that we had was to simply contact three more teams from around the globe and to only have the live discussion with them and to answer the answers from the chat. It was a fine idea and we could easily do that, but we would have lost the authenticity and creativity of the event, transforming it into an ordinary one.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ After some days of intense thinking, Catălin came up with a brilliant idea, to make to participants collaborate in order to solve problems that were a real threat to the environment and for the planet. His inspiration for this came from an event that former members of our team participated at called Youth Elite, at which teams of 10 would be given a specific place for a possible company that would help the cmmunity in a way or another.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ To get to this idea, he, at first, searched for international days with a meaning that we could use as the main theme of the event, with the closest one being the International Day of Science for Peace and Development, But we had three main issues.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ The first one was what would the theme have to do, as a robot would be quite hard to build and we were also going to start the season very soon, so the focus of the participants wasn't going to be on the event, but on their own robots for the competition. Then, the solution came to us, the goal of each team would be to designi a robat on the theme of Science for Peace and Development and to present it. The design of the robot would have to be illustrated at leas with a drawing or with a 3D model.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ The second problem actually was the presentation, as we realized that talking about each project, the way the idea came to each of the teams, how the robot would look like and about how it worked would be really hard, especially if we would do it live, so, we decided to let each of the teams present their idea in video that was going be uploaded on a Youtube channel created for the event.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ The last problem was how to get more than ten teams into an event like this, as teri teams seemed to us as a reasonable number for the first event of this type. Knowing that the robot would be presented into prerecorded videos, we decided to extend the event from one day, as we iriitially intended to make it last, to an entire week, posting a video every day.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ Another thing that we had to be careful at was that, if we invited teams from all around the globe, there would be a big time difference between same of them and it could cause a lot of problems. To avoid that, we decided that each video was going to be done by two teams that didn't have a difference bigger than three hours between their countries. With that done, the only thing we needed in arder to transform this event into reality was 13 more enthusiastic teams from around the globe that were willing to take part in this event.

26.04.2019

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ Starting with the idea that mental health amongst teenagers is very important and uncared for, we decided to create an event especially designed for youngsters that focused around this theme. Since the team functions with children between the ages of 16 and 18, this event would be beneficial for both us and the adolescents that live in Alba Iulia and close by.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ The idea began with a little talk after a meeting between the team members about the state of mind of nowadays teenagers and how stress and peer pressure affects all of us. The main problem is that students are under a lot of stress lately and that plays a big part in the day to day life. School life, what happens back home, extracurricular activities, competitions, all of these have a powerful impact over the life of young adults and sometimes it can all be too much for someone who is still developing.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ After a long thought process, we decided to get in touch with the psychologist from “1 Decembrie 1918” University so that a professional could guide us and aid us when it comes to these sort of things.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ This was an event that succeeded in improving the mental state of high school students. Our main goal- to be able to proudly say that having a mental issue isn’t something out of the orinary, that it can be fixed with a little bit of help and going to a professional isn’t shameful.

26.04.2019

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ We started with the idea that health is always the priority. However, the first to have contact with the new virus were doctors. They carry the fight for the recovery, for everyone to be safe and we decided to help them, within our powers, if not close, at least from a distance. So, together with the teams from all over the country, we mobilized ourself in Alba Iulia and, with the only printer we had at our disposal, we started printing visors. We started with lower requirements from the most affected place, the Infectious Disease Hospital where we were able to get 80 views in a short time. More than 100 visits donated to the Alba County Emergency Hospital and the COFID And DSP Alba Laboratories Teams followed. We had the 3D model of the visors, what we needed were more people to put their printers to work so we announced on our social media accounts that we give the model to anyone who is willing to help. And so collaborations followed with people from Alba Iulia who had personal printers and they also started printing. We also received help from our community, and from the CTC Store, which helped us by donating the protective film. The employees of Alba Iulia Municipality did not let themselves down and helped us to purchase the material needed to make the visors. We also managed to make our work known outside the FTC community, by participating in a video report by Laurentiu Ungureanu and Ionut Duinea for Recorder.ro, where we were invited along with other robotics teams from the country to talk about the help we could give to the doctors on the front line. We had a break period, during which our services were no longer necessary, but it seems that the situation is getting worse again and we started again to manufacture visors, this time for the House of Varstini dun Barabant, where the nurses need the best protection.

Robots to the rescue

26.04.2019

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ Robots to the rescue was a world wide competition which was hosted by PTC and had teams from different countries think about 3D modelled robots that were able to solve real world problems. Every robot gained points after a specific criteria that was based on creativity, design process, functionality, and real-life utility, bonus points being awarded to those teams who cooperated with other teams to create their robots together.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ We paired up with team RO186 The Resistance and, after some meetings at which we discussed what we could do and the actual way of transforming our ideas into reality, we got to the theme of cleaning the sea waters and shores of trash. We decided that we would design the water debris collecting robot and they would design the other one, as we already had an idea regarding this one. So, after we designed the base floating part of the robot, we got to the trash collecting and smashing mechanism and, as we knew that the regular FTC dimensions wouldn’t be enough, we designed a moving wall inside the robot that would orientate the garbage that was going inside of the robot to a certain place and, from that moment, the wall was starting to apply pressure on the debris and, from the walls, some sharp blades would start to go out and cut through the plastic so that it could be pressured more than by only getting some air out of it. Then, the pressured trash would be lifted into a compartment mounted on linear slides, which would then extend as far as 2 meters, to be able to deliver the trash to our partners on the shore, as they would collect it by standing under the compartment and it being rotated to 90 degrees so that it could be emptied.

‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ In the end we had to make a little manual of our robot that described it and it’s use, that helped us get to the 36 place at the competition out of 400 si ceva teams that entered in this competition.
